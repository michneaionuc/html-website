To Kill a Mockingbird

Back to List

Director: Robert Mulligan

In the Depression era, Atticus Finch, a lawyer, sets out to defend a Black man accused of raping a white woman. The story highlights the deep-seated prejudice and discrimination present in society, particularly through the wrongful accusation and trial of Tom Robinson, a Black man.
Additionally, it explores themes of empathy, moral growth, and the importance of understanding others, as seen through the experiences of Scout and Jem Finch as they navigate their childhood in a prejudiced world.
